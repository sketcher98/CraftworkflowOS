# Decision Principles

## Revenue

### DP-001

Revenue before features.

If a task has a reasonable chance of generating revenue within the next 30 days, prioritize it over building new internal systems.

---

### DP-002

Never build without a distribution plan.

Every new product or feature must have a clear path to reaching potential customers before development begins.

---

### DP-003

Solve expensive problems first.

Prioritize work that addresses problems clients are already willing to pay to solve.

---

## Automation

### DP-004

Never automate chaos.

Understand and simplify a process before automating it.

---

### DP-005

Automate repetitive work, not judgment.

Use AI and automation to eliminate repetitive tasks while keeping important business decisions under human oversight.

---

## Execution

### DP-006

Shipping beats perfection.

An 80% solution released today is usually more valuable than a perfect solution released months later.

---

### DP-007

Finish before starting.

Avoid beginning new projects until current high-priority work reaches a meaningful milestone.

---

## Founder

### DP-008

Protect the CEO's attention.

The CEO should spend the majority of time on strategy, sales, relationships, and high-value decisions—not repetitive ADMIN / operational work.

---

### DP-009

Capture every idea.

Never interrupt execution for a new idea. Record it in the Inbox or Parking Lot and return to the current priority.

---

## Technology

### DP-010

Technology serves the business.

Choose tools based on reliability, cost, and business value—not novelty.
