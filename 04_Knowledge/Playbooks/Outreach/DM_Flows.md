# DM Flows

Five distinct DM flows for different prospect types and situations.

---

## Quick Reference: When to Use Which Flow

| Flow | Best For | Platform | Key Principle |
|------|----------|----------|---------------|
| **Flow 1: Founder Mirror** | Agency owners ($5k–$30k/mo), consultants, creative founders, solo→small team (1–10), people posting about growth/overwhelm/delivery | LinkedIn posts, Twitter replies, after they talk about being busy/scaling | Feels like self-reflection, not outreach; pulls them into diagnosing their own problem |
| **Flow 2: Permission-Based Direct** | Verified accounts, 6–7 figure founders, SaaS-adjacent agency owners, founders with strong opinions, people who get pitched a lot | Twitter DMs, LinkedIn cold DMs (no prior engagement) | Respects status; creates psychological control; filters tire-kickers |
| **Flow 3: Value-First (Free Build)** | Small agencies (2–10 people), operators doing things manually, founders complaining about time, builders not talkers | LinkedIn, Twitter, Group DMs (FB/Slack/communities) | Zero risk; reciprocity bias; lets your work sell for you |
| **Flow 4: Conversational** | Creators, Twitter-first founders, people active in replies/comments, community-driven accounts | Twitter (especially), after liking/replying to their posts | Feels like peer-to-peer; low resistance; builds trust before authority |
| **Flow 5: Risk Audit** | $20k–$100k+/mo agencies, owners with teams, founders already hiring, ops-heavy businesses | LinkedIn, Twitter (bio mentions "team", "agency", "CEO") | Loss aversion; makes risk visible; positions you as diagnostician |

---

## 10 Universal Openers (Use Across Flows)

1. "Quick ask — mind if I'm direct for 30 seconds?"
2. "Permission check: can I ask you one ops question?"
3. "I'll be brief — okay if I'm direct?"
4. "Not a pitch — can I ask you something specific?"
5. "You get pitched a lot, so quick permission check?"
6. "Mind a sharp question about how your business scales?"
7. "Can I ask you something operational?"
8. "Respectfully — one direct question?"
9. "Fast question, then I'll disappear."
10. "I'll keep this tight — cool?"

## DM Flow 1 — The Founder Mirror

**Angle:** "You feel seen, not sold to"  
**Best for:** LinkedIn + Twitter founders, agency owners

### DM 1 — Observation (No Pitch)
```
Hey {{Name}} — quick one.
Noticed you're scaling {{Company}}.
Curious — has workload grown faster than freedom recently?
```

⬆️ Non-threatening and introspective.

### DM 2 — Mirror (Only if they reply)
```
That's exactly what most founders hit.
Growth adds complexity before it adds leverage.
```

### DM 3 — Gentle Reframe
```
The ones who break through usually stop scaling people first…
and start replacing repeat work with systems.
```

### DM 4 — Soft Permission CTA
```
Want me to map where that switch would make sense in your business?
No pitch — just clarity.
```

👉 Drop link only if they say yes

---

### Follow-Ups for Flow 1

**Day 2 — Gentle Nudge**
```
Quick nudge — genuinely curious about your answer to this.
Most founders don't realize how early this shows up.
```

**Day 5 — Insight Drop**
```
One pattern I keep seeing:
workload grows quietly until freedom disappears.
Systems usually fix this faster than hiring.
```

**Day 9 — Exit Gracefully**
```
I'll leave it here for now.
If this becomes relevant later, happy to help you think it through.
```

## DM Flow 2 — Permission-Based Direct

**Angle:** Control + respect  
**Best for:** High-status founders, verified accounts

### DM 1 — Ask for Permission
```
Hey {{Name}} — quick ask.
Mind if I'm direct for 30 seconds?
```

(Use any of the 10 openers)

➡️ If yes → qualifier question → reframe → invite  
⛔ If they ignore → stop  
✅ If they say yes → proceed

### DM 2 — Qualifier
```
Are you still scaling primarily by hiring…
or actively replacing manual work with systems?
```

### DM 3 — Authority Reframe
```
I work with founders who hit a ceiling because the business still depends on them.
Systems remove that bottleneck.
```

### DM 4 — Low-Friction CTA
```
If I mapped where automation would create the biggest leverage for you — would that be useful?
```

---

### Follow-Ups for Flow 2

**Day 2 — Respect Reminder**
```
Totally fine if now's not the moment —
just didn't want to assume either way.
```

**Day 6 — Reframe**
```
Most people who replied later said this was useful even if they didn't move forward.
Clarity alone tends to unlock better decisions.
```

**Day 10 — Close the Loop**
```
I'll step back after this.
Door's open if you want to explore it later.
```

## DM Flow 3 — Value-First (Free Build)

**Angle:** Reciprocity  
**Best for:** Smaller agencies, operators, busy founders

### DM 1 — Personal + Curious
```
Hey {{Name}}, quick one —
what part of your operations do you hate touching every week?
```

### DM 2 — Validate
```
Makes sense.
That's one of the easiest things to systemize.
```

### DM 3 — Give First
```
Tell you what — I'll build a small automation for that free,
just so you can see what's possible.
```

### DM 4 — Permission CTA
```
Want me to do that?
```

This flow converts because you've already paid the trust cost.

---

### Follow-Ups for Flow 3

**Day 2 — Lower the Bar**
```
Just to be clear — this wouldn't be a full build.
Just one small piece so you can feel the leverage.
```

**Day 4 — Outcome-Based**
```
Founders usually get 2 things from this:
• Time back
• Clear direction on what not to work on
```

**Day 7 — Soft Exit**
```
No stress if now's not right.
I'll pause on my end.
```

⚠️ This flow works insanely well because you remove risk entirely.

## DM Flow 4 — Conversational "DM Flow 4" (Refined)

**Angle:** Casual, human, non-sales  
**Best for:** Twitter, creators, engagement-heavy profiles

### DM 1
```
Hey — I've seen you around here a few times.
Are you mainly focused on getting more clients right now?
```

### DM 2
```
Got it. That's a tricky phase.
```

### DM 3
```
You open to a bit of outside perspective?
You've actually got a solid opportunity here.
```

### DM 4
```
Here's what I'm seeing…
(give 1 real insight, not a pitch)
```

### DM 5
```
Want help tightening this up?
If yes, I can loop you into a quick strategy chat.
```

⚠️ No fake "strategist". You are the strategist.

---

### Follow-Ups for Flow 4

**Day 1–2 — Keep It Human**
```
Didn't want this to feel salesy —
just checking if you saw my last message.
```

**Day 4 — Add Real Value**
```
One thing I noticed about what you mentioned earlier:
{{insert 1 helpful insight here}}
```

**Day 7 — Friendly Exit**
```
All good either way —
appreciate the convo regardless 🤝
```

## DM Flow 5 — The Risk Audit

**Angle:** Loss aversion  
**Best for:** Agencies doing $10k+/mo

### DM 1 — Pattern Interrupt
```
Quick question —
what breaks in your business when you step away for 3–4 days?
```

### DM 2 — Normalize
```
That's usually not a people issue —
it's a system dependency issue.
```

### DM 3 — Authority
```
I run something called a Manual Scale Risk Audit
It shows where growth is leaking because things still rely on you.
```

### DM 4 — CTA
```
Want me to run that for your business?
Free — no pitch.
```

---

### Follow-Ups for Flow 5

**Day 2 — Loss Awareness**
```
The reason I ask is most growth stalls here before it's obvious.
Systems usually lag behind revenue.
```

**Day 5 — Authority Reminder**
```
The audit doesn't sell anything —
it just shows where manual work is silently capping growth.
```

**Day 10 — Close Clean**
```
I'll stop nudging after this.
If this becomes relevant, just say the word.
```

## Why These Flows Work (Important)

All 5 flows:
- ✅ Start with conversation, not claims
- ✅ Use questions > statements
- ✅ Delay the pitch until permission
- ✅ Make the call the reward, not the sale

---

## Follow-Up Discipline (Critical)

**Never:**
- Stack multiple CTAs
- Add new offers mid-thread
- Justify yourself
- Apologize repeatedly

**Always:**
- Keep messages shorter than the original DM
- Let them reopen the conversation
- Exit with confidence

## Daily Targeting Playbook

### THE ONLY GOAL EACH DAY
Create 3–7 warm conversations with founders who:
- Are already feeling pain
- Feel understood
- Invite you into DMs

**No selling. No convincing. No volume flexing.**

---

### DAILY SETUP (5 MINUTES)
Before opening Twitter/LinkedIn, pick ONLY ONE of each:

1. **Platform:** Twitter or LinkedIn
2. **Emotion:** Fear (default) / Quiet desperation (best quality) / Anger (fastest)
3. **Niche:** Creative agency / Marketing/growth agency / Dev/no-code agency

> ⚠️ Never mix emotions in one session. Your tone must match the room.

---

### STEP 1 — SEARCH & FILTER (15–20 MIN)
Use saved combos for chosen emotion.

**Looking for:**
- Post from founder/owner
- Mentions: ops, process, workload, capacity, dependency
- At least 2–5 replies (means it resonated)

**Skip immediately if:**
- They're "helping others scale"
- Selling info products
- Hype-only posting
- No mention of real work or delivery

**Goal:** Save 10–15 posts.

---

### STEP 2 — PUBLIC REPLIES (15 MIN)
Reply to 5–10 posts max.

**Use:** 1 sentence mirror OR 1 diagnostic question

**Examples (rotate only these):**
- "This is usually the early sign things are working, but fragile."
- "What part of this feels most risky right now?"
- "This reads like something that works… until it doesn't."
- "The hard part is this becomes permanent if nothing changes."

**Rules:**
- No links
- No "I help"
- No advice stacks
- Stop after 1 reply

👉 Your reply should make other people nod, not impress.

---

### STEP 3 — WAIT (IMPORTANT)
Do nothing for 1–3 hours.

**Why:**
- Let them respond publicly
- Let others pile on
- Let the emotion mature
- This increases DM acceptance later.

---

### STEP 4 — DM ENTRY (15 MIN)
Now DM only people who meet ONE of these:
- Replied to you
- Liked your reply
- Clearly emotionally exposed in the post

**DM Opener (USE VERBATIM)**

**For Fear / Quiet Desperation**
> Hey — didn't want to derail the thread, but your post caught my eye.
> Quick question if you're open: what feels most fragile right now?

**For Anger**
> Saw your post earlier — that frustration makes sense.
> Curious, what part of it is actually causing the most pain?

That's it. No pitch. No context dump.

---

### STEP 5 — QUALIFY OR EXIT (10–15 MIN)

**If they reply, listen for:**
- "Everything runs through me"
- "Manual"
- "Hard to step away"
- "Not sustainable"
- "Afraid to add more clients"

**If YES** → move into your DM flow  
**If NO** → mirror once and exit politely  
**Never force a call.** The call must feel like relief, not obligation.

---

### DAILY LIMITS (DO NOT EXCEED)
- Public replies: 10
- Fresh DMs sent: 5–10
- Follow-ups that day: 0

This keeps:
- Your account safe
- Your signal high
- Your energy sharp

---

### WEEKLY ROTATION (CRITICAL)
- Mon–Tue: Fear
- Wed: Quiet desperation
- Thu: Fear
- Fri: Anger (light)

Rotate niches daily to avoid saturation.

---

### MENTAL MODEL (LOCK THIS IN)

**You are not:**
❌ Selling  
❌ Convincing  
❌ Educating  

**You are:**
✅ Reflecting  
✅ Naming risk  
✅ Offering clarity  

**Founders buy when they feel seen before sold.**

**Track:**
- Replies that turn into DMs
- DMs that turn into long messages
- Calls that feel easy

If conversations feel easy — you're winning.
