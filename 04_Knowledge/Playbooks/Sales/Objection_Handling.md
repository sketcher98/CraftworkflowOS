# Objection Handling

8 common objections with exact responses.

---

## Objection 1: "I need to think about it"

**Translation:** "I don't feel safe deciding yet."

**Response:**
> "Totally fair.
> 
> Quick question so I don't misread this —
> do you need more clarity, or do you need more time?"

Then shut up.

- **If clarity:** "What part feels unclear — the problem, the solution, or the scope?" → Diagnose → "Does that clear it up enough to decide?"
- **If time:** "That makes sense. Just so we're aligned — what specifically are you thinking through?"
  - If they can't answer clearly → it's clarity, not time

---

## Objection 2: "It's too expensive"

**Translation:** "I don't see the ROI yet."

**Response:**
> "Totally understand.
> 
> Compared to what though — hiring, staying manual, or continuing as is?"

Let them answer.

> "If this removes you as the system and frees up 80+ hours/month, would the price still be the blocker?"

- If they say yes → disqualify politely
- If they say no → it's trust, not price

> "Right now, you're already paying — XXXX
> just in time, stress, and lost opportunities instead of systems."

**Goat Farmer Reframe:**
> "Ok... See it like this, Your goats are escaping because the fence is broken.
> I can fix the farm fence — or you can keep chasing goats.
> 
> Hint... It's cheaper to fix the fence once than to keep replacing goats every month."

---

## Objection 3: "I need to talk to my partner / team"

**Translation:** "I'm unsure how to explain this."

**Response:**
> "Of course.
> 
> When you explain this to them,
> what part do you think they'll push back on?"

This surfaces the real objection.

> "Want me to send a short breakdown you can forward,
> or would it help if they joined a quick call?"

---

## Objection 4: "We'll do it in-house"

**Translation:** "We want control — but haven't thought it through."

**Response:**
> "Just so we're clear —
> are you trying to remove yourself as the system,
> or just make the current one slightly less painful?"

**CLIENT:** ...

> "Makes sense.
> Do you already have someone designing system architecture — or just executing tasks?"

If they say executing tasks:

> "Got it.
> That's usually where founders end up hiring to compensate —
> which works short term, but locks in monthly cost instead of removing the problem.
> 
> The real question is whether you want to manage that system —
> or be removed from it entirely."

---

## Objection 5: "We've tried automation before and it didn't work"

**Translation:** "I don't trust the process."

**Response:**
> "That's actually very common.
> 
> Most automations fail because they automate chaos
> instead of fixing the system underneath."
> 
> "Quick check — was it tool-first or system-first?"

Almost always: tool-first.

**Authority anchor:**
> "We don't automate tasks.
> We design systems — then automate them."

---

## Objection 6: "Now isn't the right time"

**Translation:** "I'm overwhelmed."

**Response (TIME PARADOX):**
> "Totally get that.
> 
> But just to be clear —
> is now busy because things are manual,
> or manual because things are busy?"
> 
> That question lands.
> 
> "This is usually the exact moment systems help most —
> not later."
> 
> "So the real question is — do you want relief now, or more pressure later?"

---

## Objection 7: Silence / Non-Answers / Deflection

**Translation:** "I'm processing."

**Move:** Shut up & Count 3 seconds in your head.

> "What's coming up for you right now?"

---

## Objection 8: CLEAN EXIT (if no decision)

> "No pressure.
> If replacing manual scale with systems becomes a priority later, feel free to reach out."
> 
> Pause.
> 
> "Either way, I hope you escape the hiring trap."

## Core Principles

1. **Never chase** — You allow, never chase
2. **Never defend** — You reframe, never defend
3. **Never justify** — You reframe, never justify
4. **Silence is power** — Count 3 seconds before responding
5. **Questions > Statements** — Let them convince themselves
6. **Clean exit builds trust** — "Either path is valid — one just compounds faster"
