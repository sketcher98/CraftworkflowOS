## Roleplay 1: The Skeptical, Time-Poor Founder

**Profile:**
- Already successful ($30–50k/mo)
- Overwhelmed
- Hates "sales calls"
- Low patience
- High ego, but reasonable

---

### 🧠 OPENING

**YOU:**
> "Before we start, quick context.
> This isn't a sales call.
> 
> We'll look at how your business actually runs day-to-day, where things break when you step away, and whether systems can realistically fix it.
> 
> If this isn't something I can fix cleanly (and predictably), I'll tell you straight.
> Sound fair?"

**ME (CLIENT):**
"Yeah, sure. But just so you know, I only have about 20 minutes."

---

### 🧠 DISCOVERY

**YOU:**
> "Walk me through what happens from a new lead coming in → client delivery."

**ME:**
"Leads come in, sales follows up, we close, ops takes over. Pretty standard."
*(Neutral. Not giving you much.)*

**YOU (probe):**
> "And when this is working the way you want, what does your role look like day-to-day?"

**ME:**
"I shouldn't be involved at all… but realistically I still am."

**YOU:**
> "How many hours are you personally involved then?"

**ME:**
"Too many. Maybe 20 hours a week? Depends."

**YOU:**
> "What breaks when you're busy or unavailable?"

**ME:**
"Follow-up slips. Things don't move unless I check in. Stuff falls through cracks."

---

### 🧠 TEST POINT (IMPORTANT)

**YOU (label):**
> "So what I'm hearing is — the business doesn't break because of volume… it breaks because everything still routes through you. Sound accurate?"

**ME:**
"…yeah. That's fair."

✅ You have buy-in. Keep control.

---

### 🧠 COST

**YOU:**
> "Let me pressure-test this with you. How many hours a week does this cost you?"

**ME:**
"Like I said… 15–20."

**YOU:**
> "If nothing changes for 6 months, what does that cost you in energy or missed revenue?"

**ME:**
"I'll probably burn out or cap growth. But that's business."

---

### ⚠️ Hard Pushback #1

**ME (leans back):**
"Look — I get the idea. But honestly? This is just the cost of running a business."

---

### ✅ YOUR ACQ RESPONSE

> "You're right — most founders accept that as 'the cost of business.'
> 
> The issue is what you're paying for.
> 
> Hiring is paying every month to manage the same problem.
> Systems are paying once to remove it."
> 
> ⏸️ Pause.
> 
> Then immediately turn the knife gently:
> 
> "So the real question isn't whether this costs money.
> 
> It's whether you want to keep paying for supervision —
> or pay once to stop being involved."

---

### 🔥 NOW I PUSH BACK HARDER (REALISTIC)

**ME (CLIENT):**
"Sure, but I already have people.
They're doing fine.
I just need them to be a bit more disciplined."

---

### ✅ YOUR RESPONSE

> "That makes sense — most founders think it's a discipline issue at first."
> 
> ⏸️ (Micro-validation)
> 
> "The tricky part is… even with good people, everything still routes through you.
> So instead of doing the work, you're supervising the work."
> 
> ⏸️ (Let that land)
> 
> "That exact pattern — founder as the system — is what I see right before people either burn out or start hiring their way into more complexity."
> 
> Then pin it to them (this is the missing piece):
> 
> "And based on what you told me earlier, that's already starting here."
> 
> ⏸️ Silence.

---

### 🔥 NOW I PUSH BACK HARDER (REAL CLOSE)

**ME (CLIENT):**
"Okay, but honestly… I don't mind being involved.
This is my business. I like control."

---

### ✅ YOUR RESPONSE

> "Totally get that — wanting control isn't the problem."
> 
> ⏸️ (Validation without agreement)
> 
> "The issue is how you're controlling things right now."
> 
> ⏸️
> 
> "At the moment, control means you're involved in decisions, follow-ups, and supervision."
> 
> ⏸️
> 
> "With systems, control shifts — you're not in the weeds anymore, but nothing moves without your rules."
> 
> ⏸️
> 
> "So you don't lose control.
> You just stop paying for it with your time."
> 
> ⏸️ Silence.

---

### 🔥 FINAL HARD PUSHBACK (CLOSER-LEVEL)

**ME (CLIENT):**
"Alright. This all makes sense.
I just don't think now is the right time."

---

### ✅ YOUR RESPONSE

> "Totally get that.
> 
> But just to be clear —
> is now busy because things are manual,
> or manual because things are busy?"
> 
> ⏸️ They answer.
> 
> Then add this:
> 
> "Because if it's the second one, waiting usually just makes it heavier."
> 
> Then stop talking.

## Roleplay 1.1: Hostile / Skeptical Founder

**Scenario:**
- Burned by consultants
- Thinks "automation is overhyped"
- Doesn't trust agencies

---

### 🧑‍💼 PROSPECT (ME)

> "Look, I'll be honest.
> Every automation person I've talked to promises freedom.
> What actually happens is more tools and more complexity."

---

### ✅ YOUR ACQ RESPONSE

> (Calm. Slow.)
> 
> "That makes sense.
> 
> Can I ask — when you tried automation before,
> was it designed around your system…
> or was it mostly tools stitched together?"
> 
> Then stop talking.

---

### 🧑‍💼 PROSPECT

> "Tools. Zapier, CRMs, dashboards… it just became another thing to manage."

---

### ✅ YOUR RESPONSE (AUTHORITY + REFRAME)

> "Yeah — that's the common failure point.
> 
> That's not automation failing.
> That's chaos being automated."
> 
> (Pause)
> 
> "We don't automate tasks.
> We design the system first — then automate it."

---

### 🧑‍💼 PROSPECT

> "Okay, but I still don't see how this doesn't end with me babysitting it."

---

### ✅ YOUR RESPONSE (FRAME HOLD)

> "Fair question.
> 
> Right now, are you already babysitting people, processes, or follow-ups?"
> 
> (Let them answer.)
> 
> Then:
> 
> "So the real question isn't whether you'll supervise something —
> it's whether you want to supervise people forever,
> or install a system once and step out of it."
> 
> Then silence.

---

### 🧑‍💼 PROSPECT

> "Hmm. I still need to think about it."

---

### ✅ YOUR RESPONSE (ACQ CLARITY SPLIT)

> "Totally fair.
> 
> Quick check so I don't misread this —
> do you need more clarity, or more time?"
> 
> (Do not rescue them.)

---

### 🧑‍💼 PROSPECT

> "Probably clarity."

---

### ✅ YOUR RESPONSE

> "Got it.
> 
> What part feels unclear —
> the problem, the solution, or the scope?"

> At this point, you're back in control.
> No defensiveness. No pitch mode.

## Key Takeaways from All Roleplays

### The ACQ Method

1. **Frame Control** — You set the agenda, not them
2. **Silence is Power** — Count 3 seconds before responding
3. **Questions > Statements** — Let them convince themselves
4. **Label, Don't Solve** — "So things route back to you" not "Here's the fix"
5. **Clean Exit Builds Trust** — "Either path is valid — one just compounds faster"
6. **Never Chase** — You allow, never chase
7. **Never Defend** — You reframe, never defend
8. **Never Justify** — You reframe, never justify

### The ACQ Phrases That Work

| Situation | Phrase |
|-----------|--------|
| Time pressure | "If we don't see a clear way to remove you as the bottleneck, we'll end early anyway. Sound fair?" |
| No pain admitted | "If nothing's broken, what made this call worth taking?" |
| Price objection | "Compared to what — hiring, staying manual, or continuing as is?" |
| "Send proposal" | "What decision would that proposal help you make?" |
| "Think about it" | "Do you need more clarity, or more time?" |
| "Too expensive" | "If this removes you as the system and frees 80+ hrs/mo, is price still the blocker?" |
| "Already profitable" | "Is the goal to stay profitable, or to scale without adding pressure?" |
| "Burned before" | "Was it execution, or no one fixed the underlying system?" |
| "Risky" | "What's riskier — changing what's breaking, or staying the bottleneck?" |
| Clean exit | "Either path is valid — one just compounds faster" |

### The Goat Farmer Reframe

> "Ok... See it like this, Your goats are escaping because the fence is broken.
> I can fix the farm fence — or you can keep chasing goats.
> 
> Hint... It's cheaper to fix the fence once than to keep replacing goats every month."
