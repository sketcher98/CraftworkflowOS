# Sales Call Playbook

Complete sales call script with frame control, discovery, cost, reframe, offer, and close.

---

## Pre-Call Sequence

### 4-Email Pre-Call Sequence

**EMAIL 1 — IMMEDIATE (Confirmation + Relief)**
- Subject: "Your Automation Escape Audit is booked"
- Confirms booking, sets expectations: working session, not sales call
- Asks: "If nothing changes, what does your business look like in 12 months?"

**EMAIL 2 — +24 HOURS (Authority + Story)**
- Subject: "Why I shut down my agency"
- Origin story: ran service business, hiring didn't fix it, automation was the exit

**EMAIL 3 — +48 HOURS (Belief Shift)**
- Subject: "Hiring isn't scaling anymore"
- Belief shift: winners use fewer people, stronger systems, higher leverage

**EMAIL 4 — 2–4 HOURS BEFORE CALL (Show-Up Trigger)**
- Subject: "Quick reminder (read this)"
- Bring: current tools, main bottleneck, one workflow you hate

**OPTIONAL: SMS/WHATSAPP NUDGE (1 hour before)**
- "Hey {{Name}}, looking forward to our Automation Escape Audit in 1 hour. This won't be a sales call — just clarity. See you soon. — Precious"
- Can double show-up rate

## Call Structure Overview

| Phase | Time | Purpose |
|-------|------|---------|
| **Opening / Frame Control** | 0–5 min | Set frame, establish authority, get permission |
| **Discovery / Find Friction** | 5–15 min | Walk through workflow, find bottlenecks |
| **Cost / Make It Real** | 15–25 min | Quantify cost of inaction |
| **Reframe / Introduce New Way** | 25–30 min | Connect belief → solution |
| **Offer / Next Logical Step** | 30–40 min | Recommend ONE package, guarantee |
| **Close / Control Without Pressure** | 40–45 min | Binary choice, clean exit if needed |

## Opening — Frame Control (0–5 min)

```
"Before we start, quick context.
This isn't a sales call.

We'll look at how your business actually runs day-to-day, where things break when you step away, and whether systems can realistically fix it.

If this isn't something I can fix cleanly (and predictably), I'll tell you straight.
Sound fair?"
```

**CLIENT:** ...

⛔ If they say no → red flag  
✅ If they say yes → you're in control

---

## Discovery — Find the Friction (5–15 min)

**Ask slowly. Let them talk.**

### Core Questions (don't rush)

1. **"Walk me through what happens from a new lead coming in → client delivery."**
   - Let them map the entire workflow

2. **"And when this is working the way you want… what does your role look like day-to-day?"**
   - Ideal vs. reality

### Probe Questions (go deep)

- **"What are you still personally involved in that shouldn't require you?"**
- **"How many hours are you personally involved then?"**
- **"Where do things slow down?"**
- **"What still needs manual follow-up?"**
- **"What breaks when you're busy or unavailable?"**

### Label & Confirm

Say ONE of these (pick ONE, not all):

> "And when that happens — what does it force you to do personally?"
> 
> OR
> 
> "What usually gets sacrificed when that breaks — revenue, sleep, or focus?"

**CLIENT:** ...

> "So what I'm hearing is — the business doesn't really break because of volume… it breaks because everything still routes through you.
> That sound accurate?"

**CLIENT:** ...

> "If we could remove this bottleneck without adding headcount or complexity, would that be worth fixing for you?"

- If yes → green light
- If hesitation → uncover objection early
- If no → disqualify fast

⚠️ **Do not solve yet. Just label problems.**

## Cost — Make It Real (15–25 min)

**This is where the sale is won.**

```
"Let me pressure-test this with you."
```

### Questions

1. **"Roughly how many hours a week does this pull you back in?"**
2. **"If nothing changes for 6 months, what does that cost you — in energy, missed revenue, or growth?"** >> Let it sit <<
3. **"If nothing changed, how long could you realistically keep operating like this before it becomes a problem?"**
4. **"With the current setup, what does growth actually look like?"**
5. **"And if volume doubles, does this get easier — or harder?"**

### Reflect Back

> "So growth actually increases pressure, not freedom."

**CLIENT:** ...

> "So, staying manual here is costing you roughly ___ per month and still caps growth. Is that accurate?"

**CLIENT:** ... They say yes. They just convinced themselves.

> "And you're the only reason it still works at all — fair?"

**CLIENT:** ...

### The Hiring Trap

PAUSE. Hmm (2–3 seconds)

> "Most founders try to solve this by hiring — a VA or ops person.
> That's $1–3k every month, forever."
> 
> "And that's assuming they're good — because bad hires don't just cost money, they slow everything down."

PAUSE AGAIN (1–2 seconds). Then immediately follow with:

> "And the tricky part is… even after hiring, you're still the system.
> You're just supervising the system now."

Let that land.

Then anchor Burnout:

> "That's exactly where most founders burn out — not from effort, but from preventable friction."
> 
> "You see... This exact pattern — everything routing through the founder — is what I see right before people either burn out or start hiring their way into more complexity."

## Reframe — Introduce the New Way (25–30 min)

### Connect Belief → Solution

> "The issue isn't effort or discipline."
> 
> "It's that your business still depends on you as the system."

**Say this slowly. No bullets. No rush.**

> "Here's the shift most founders never make.
> 
> Right now, coordination is manual.
> 
> With systems, decisions trigger actions automatically.
> 
> That's the difference."

Pause again.

> "So your role stops being 'operator'.
> It becomes 'owner'."

> "Six months from now, the difference is simple:
> deals move without you chasing,
> follow up & onboarding happens without reminders,
> and you're looking at a dashboard — not your inbox — to know what's happening."

### For Analytical/Numbers-Driven Clients

> "Think of it like this.
> 
> People scale linearly.
> Systems scale once.
> 
> Right now, growth means more coordination, more supervision, more decisions from you. And you step in when something breaks.
> 
> With systems, growth just increases volume — not involvement.
> 
> You don't manage work anymore.
> You manage outcomes."

### Goat Farmer Analogy (Warm + Nodding)

> "It's the same reason goat farmers build fences instead of hiring more people to chase goats.
> 
> One fixes the problem permanently.
> The other just reacts to it every day."

Then stop talking.

## Offer — The Next Logical Step (30–40 min)

```
"Earlier, you said the real issue isn't effort —
it's that everything still routes through you."

(Pause)

"If we remove that,
everything else gets easier."

(Pause)

"The cleanest way to remove yourself as the bottleneck
is installing a self-running revenue system here first."

Then recommend ONE package:

"That's exactly what our [Goldilocks / Visionary] setup is built for."

"And just to be clear — if this doesn't remove you as the bottleneck the way we define it today, we keep working on it until it does. That's on me."
```

### Binary Choice

> "At this point, there are two options.
> 
> Option A: keep things manual and pay the cost in time & stress.
> Option B: install Self-Running systems once and remove the drag.
> 
> Which makes more sense for you? If we move forward, we'd start within the next 2-3 days while this context is fresh.
> 
> Then stop talking. Silence = power."

## Close — Control Without Pressure (40–45 min)

### If They Hesitate

> "Totally fine tho.
> Either path is valid — one just compounds wayyy faster."

### If They Ask Price

> "Given what this removes and what it replaces,
> does this feel like a priority to fix now —
> or something to delay?"

**You never chase. You allow.**

## Objection Handling

### 1. "I need to think about it"
**Translation:** "I don't feel safe deciding yet."

**Response:**
> "Totally fair.
> Quick question so I don't misread this —
> do you need more clarity, or do you need more time?"

Then shut up.

- **If clarity:** "What part feels unclear — the problem, the solution, or the scope?" → Diagnose → "Does that clear it up enough to decide?"
- **If time:** "That makes sense. Just so we're aligned — what specifically are you thinking through?"
  - If they can't answer clearly → it's clarity, not time

### 2. "It's too expensive"
**Translation:** "I don't see the ROI yet."

**Response:**
> "Totally understand.
> Compared to what though — hiring, staying manual, or continuing as is?"

Let them answer.

> "If this removes you as the system and frees up 80+ hours/month, would the price still be the blocker?"

- If they say yes → disqualify politely
- If they say no → it's trust, not price

> "Right now, you're already paying — XXXX
> just in time, stress, and lost opportunities instead of systems."

**Goat Farmer Reframe:**
> "Ok... See it like this, Your goats are escaping because the fence is broken.
> I can fix the farm fence — or you can keep chasing goats.
> 
> Hint... It's cheaper to fix the fence once than to keep replacing goats every month."

### 3. "I need to talk to my partner / team"
**Translation:** "I'm unsure how to explain this."

**Response:**
> "Of course.
> When you explain this to them,
> what part do you think they'll push back on?"

This surfaces the real objection.

> "Want me to send a short breakdown you can forward,
> or would it help if they joined a quick call?"

### 4. "We'll do it in-house"
**Translation:** "We want control — but haven't thought it through."

**Response:**
> "Just so we're clear —
> are you trying to remove yourself as the system,
> or just make the current one slightly less painful?"

**CLIENT:** ...

> "Makes sense.
> Do you already have someone designing system architecture — or just executing tasks?"

If they say executing tasks:

> "Got it.
> That's usually where founders end up hiring to compensate —
> which works short term, but locks in monthly cost instead of removing the problem.
> 
> The real decision is whether you want to manage that system —
> or be removed from it entirely."

### 5. "We've tried automation before and it didn't work"
**Translation:** "I don't trust the process."

**Response:**
> "That's actually very common.
> Most automations fail because they automate chaos
> instead of fixing the system underneath."
> 
> "Quick check — was it tool-first or system-first?"
> 
> Almost always: tool-first.
> 
> **Authority anchor:**
> "We don't automate tasks.
> We design systems — then automate them."

### 6. "Now isn't the right time"
**Translation:** "I'm overwhelmed."

**Response (TIME PARADOX):**
> "Totally get that.
> But just to be clear —
> is now busy because things are manual,
> or manual because things are busy?"
> 
> That question lands.
> 
> "This is usually the exact moment systems help most —
> not later."
> 
> "So the real question is — do you want relief now, or more pressure later?"

### 7. Silence / Non-Answers / Deflection
**Translation:** "I'm processing."

**Move:** Shut up & Count 3 seconds in your head.

> "What's coming up for you right now?"

### 8. CLEAN EXIT (if no decision)

> "No pressure.
> If replacing manual scale with systems becomes a priority later, feel free to reach out."
> 
> Pause.
> 
> "Either way, I hope you escape the hiring trap."

## Returning Prospect Mini-Script

### 1️⃣ OPEN — RECLAIM FRAME (1–2 min)

> "Good to see you again.
> 
> Before we dive in —
> this isn't a re-pitch.
> 
> I just want to understand what changed since we last spoke,
> and see if this actually makes sense now."

### 2️⃣ CHANGE DETECTION (2–3 min)

> "Last time we spoke, the main issue was that everything still routed through you.
> 
> What's different now?"

Shut up. Let them talk.

Common: "Volume picked up", "Things broke", "I'm back in the weeds", "Follow-ups are slipping", "I thought we could handle it, but…"

Do not interrupt.

### 3️⃣ MIRROR + TIME COLLAPSE (2 min)

> "Got it.
> 
> So the business didn't really break —
> it just routed back to you again."

Pause. Let them confirm.

> "This is basically the same issue we mapped before —
> it just got louder with more volume."

### 4️⃣ DECISION CHECK (1–2 min)

> "So let me ask you directly.
> 
> Are you trying to remove yourself as the system now —
> or are you just looking for temporary relief?"

Silence. Binary ownership question.

### 5️⃣ OFFER (NO RESELL) (2 min)

> "Nothing about the solution has changed.
> 
> If you want to remove yourself as the system,
> installing a self-running revenue setup still does that.
> 
> If now's the time, we can move forward.
> If not, we pause again."

Stop talking.

### 6️⃣ CLOSE (IF THEY SAY YES)

> "Cool.
> 
> We'll start with the same scope we discussed before,
> focus on removing you as the bottleneck first,
> and build from there.
> 
> I'll send the next steps."
